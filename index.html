Transactions
  • Patient visits hospital/ patient brought by an ambulance in case of emergency.
  • Patient describes the reason of coming/ treatment required to the hospital staff./in case of emergency the doctors examine the patient immediately.
  • Hospital staff will call the respective doctor on-call for the specified treatment
  • Patient is examined by on-call doctors
  • Patient hands over documents of any previous hospitalization or major allergies and illnesses, which is then examined by the doctor
  • Attending Doctors decides whether the patient will be an inpatient or outpatient.
  • If inpatient, attending doctor prepares patient file with treatment given.
  • Attending doctor must write an admission note.
  • Patient/guardian must show the admission note to the reception and pay the deposit based on the type of room the patient wants. In case of emergency patients the patients are directly admitted for treatment/operation if necessary. In case the patient has insurance accepted by the hospital, the deposit is not needed.
  • In case of emergency patients, after a successful surgery the patient is taken to the general ward or icu if required under the doctors orders. The patient information is filed hereafter
  • The staff Obtains the patient's insurance information and the patient spouse's insurance information if applicable. Obtain Photo Identification, scan insurance information, Photo ID and any other information if applicable.
  • The nurse performs complete assessment of the patient and enter patient name, date and time of admission, chief complaints, medical diagnosis in the admission file or patient file.
  • Hospital staff enters the patient's demographic and insurance information, the admitting physician and the diagnosis completely and accurately in the computer system.
  • If the patient's demographic and insurance information is present in the computer system, verify all information and update as needed.
  • Review the Consent for Treatment with the patient.
  • Have the patient or patient's legal representative sign the Consent for Treatment. Legal guardians must produce legal documentation .A minor under the age of 18 must have a parent sign for treatment.
  • Then Patient is taken to their respective room, and the nurses/ medical staff start the treatment based on the doctors prescription.
  • The Admitting Clerk will give the patient a HIPAA Notice of Privacy Practice. and a Friends, Family,Visitor Notification.
  • Reiterate to the patient that any valuable should be sent home with family members or be kept in the hospital safe.
  • The computer will generate the Admission Summary Sheet (face sheet) and Consent for Treatment
  • The hospital staff will take all completed and signed paperwork to the Nurses station and assemble their chart.
